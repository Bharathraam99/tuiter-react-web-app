    Lemons are a citrus fruit that grow on trees and are native to Asia. They are widely cultivated for their acidic juice, which is used in cooking, baking, and as a flavoring in drinks. Lemons are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants, which provide numerous health benefits. They are also a source of dietary fiber and contain minerals such as calcium, potassium, and magnesium. In addition to their culinary uses, lemons have been used for medicinal purposes for centuries, with claims ranging from curing scurvy to aiding digestion and improving skin health. Lemons are a versatile and flavorful fruit that add a refreshing tartness to many dishes and beverages.
